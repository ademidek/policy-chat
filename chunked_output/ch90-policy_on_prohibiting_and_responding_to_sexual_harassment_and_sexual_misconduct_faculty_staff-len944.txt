university of richmond | 12
gov-3104 –policy on prohibiting and responding to sexual harassment / sexual
misconduct –faculty and staff
the following:
o the seriousness of the alleged sexual harassment/sexual misconduct;
o circumstances indicating increased risk of additional acts by the respondent;
o the complainant's reasonable safety concerns regarding the initiation of a complaint;
o the basis for the complainant's request not to proceed;
o prior complaints against the respondent;
o any history of the respondent's arrests;
o threats from the respondent;
o the involvement of multiple alleged perpetrators;
o any pattern of perpetration via drugs or alcohol at a given location or by a given group;
o the age of complainant; and
o the ability of the university to gather relevant evidence. the title ix coordinator may consult with the sexual misconduct review subcommittee and
other university officials when considering these factors.
