data stewards are responsible for all of the following for data within their data
stewardship domain:
• developing and documenting data standards
• documenting and annually updating data integrations and partnering with information services to
map third party data sources and destinations (effective may 1, 2024)
• establishing the business rules and business processes for their respective data stewardship
domains
• establishing the business rules and business processes for the transactional systems associated
with their data stewardship domain
• ensuring compliance with federal regulations, regional accreditation standards, and other rules
and regulations associated with management of the data in their data stewardship doman (e.g.,
gdpr, ferpa, glba, pci) and providing associated training
• ensuring data privacy (e.g., should we collect this data?) and data security (e.g., confidentiality)
• monitoring/overseeing data sharing (e.g., data exports, reporting, data integrations)
• adjudicating requests to access unit record or aggregate data within data stewardship domain
• complying with university records retention policy
• reviewing data access permissions annually
• understanding integrations and their impacts across related data stewardship domains
• partnering with information services to review and approve new systems or data integrations that
impact data
• acting as data ambassadors to communicate data and process changes • implementation of this
policy.
